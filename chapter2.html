Chart Types and Construction

Understanding the foundation of technical visualization

Financial charts are the visual language of technical analysis, providing traders and analysts with the tools to interpret market behavior, identify patterns, and make informed trading decisions. Each chart type offers unique perspectives on price action and market sentiment.

Line Chart

Simple trend visualization

The most basic chart type, connecting closing prices with a continuous line. Ideal for identifying overall trends and long-term price movements.

Clean, easy to read
Great for trend identification
Lacks intraday detail

Bar Chart (OHLC)

Comprehensive price data

Displays Open, High, Low, and Close prices for each period, providing complete price action data in a compact vertical bar format.

Complete OHLC data
Compact representation
Requires interpretation

Candlestick Chart

Visual pattern recognition

Originating from 18th-century Japanese rice traders, candlesticks offer enhanced visual impact with colored bodies that make patterns immediately recognizable.

Visual pattern recognition
Rich market sentiment data
Most popular among traders

Candlestick Anatomy

Understanding the building blocks of candlestick analysis

Bullish Candlestick

Close price is higher than open price (green/white body)

Bearish Candlestick

Close price is lower than open price (red/black body)

Key Components

  • Real Body: Price range between open and close
  • Upper Shadow: High price above the body
  • Lower Shadow: Low price below the body
